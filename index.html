POC Framework & Objectives
Problem Statement
TMA's current crew scheduling processes require optimization to address operational inefficiencies and resource constraints. The POC was designed to validate vendor capabilities across three critical dimensions:
1
Automated Daily Rostering
Development of an automated scheduling model operating within defined operational constraints to eliminate manual processes
2
Annual Crew Planning Optimization
Comprehensive gap analysis of TMA's historical annual crew planning with optimized annual plan of 2025 along with a 3-month training plan
3
Full Scope Solution Design
Detailed implementation planning including realistic cost projections, timelines, and resource requirements for enterprise deployment
Evaluation Criteria
Technical Validation
  • 100% alignment to hard constraints with execution time under 30 minutes
  • Daily auto-rostering solution performance and accuracy with more than 95 % of the Flights auto assigned
  • Minimizing crew utilization and reducing swap requirements
  • Annual planning methodology and optimization approach
Business Alignment
  • Understanding of TMA's operational environment
  • Alignment with business requirements and constraints
  • Change management and implementation readiness
Commercial Viability
  • Total cost of ownership and pricing transparency
  • Implementation timeline feasibility
  • Long-term partnership potential
